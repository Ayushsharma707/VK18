ODI (2008-Present)

Virat Kohli debuted for India in 2008 and quickly rose to prominence as one of cricket's most reliable run-scorers. T20s (2010-2024)

Virat Kohli made his T20I debut for India in 2010 and quickly established himself as one of the format's most consistent and prolific performers. Known for his aggressive yet calculated approach, Kohli became a vital player for India in the shortest format. His ability to chase down totals and his adaptability across different conditions made him a standout performer. Kohli is often regarded as one of the best T20I batsmen of all time, winning multiple accolades for his consistency and performances in key tournaments. In 2024, Kohli led India to victory in the T20 World Cup, earning the Man of the Match award in the final, after which he retired from T20Is.

Test (2011-Present)

Virat Kohli made his Test debut for India in 2011 and soon established himself as a top-order force. His peak came between 2016 and 2019, during which he consistently scored centuries and led India to numerous Test victories both at home and abroad. Kohli's aggressive captaincy and batting dominance earned him accolades, including the ICC Test Player of the Year. He is considered one of the finest Test batsmen of his era.

IPL (2008-PRESENT)

Virat Kohli is one of the greatest batsmen in IPL history. Since his debut in 2008 with Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB), Kohli has consistently demonstrated his dominance in the tournament. He holds the record for the most runs in IPL history, with 8,004 runs to his name. His ability to adapt to different situations and consistently deliver has made him a standout player. Kohli has won the prestigious Orange Cap twice, awarded to the tournament's leading run-scorer, a testament to his prolific run-making ability. In the 2016 season, he set an unparalleled record, scoring 973 runs in a single season. He also holds the record for the most centuries in the IPL, having scored 8 centuries, further cementing his legacy as one of the league's all-time greats..

PLAYING STYLE
He is not just a king...He is a genius!!!

Virat Kohli, a modern cricketing great, is known for his aggressive and technically sound playing style. His approach combines power with precision, making him a formidable batsman across all formats.

Aggressive Batting

Kohli’s aggressive mindset is a hallmark of his batting. He excels in scoring quickly and dominating bowlers. His strike rotation involves playing singles and doubles regularly, which keeps the scoreboard moving and places continuous pressure on bowlers. Kohli also has a knack for boundary scoring; he frequently finds gaps and hits boundaries through various shots such as cover drives and pull shots.

Technical Mastery

Despite his aggressive approach, Kohli possesses exceptional technical skills. His technique is a blend of traditional and modern elements. He maintains a balanced stance that allows him to effectively play both front-foot and back-foot shots. His ability to use a straight bat is crucial for executing straight drives and solid defense, while his precise footwork helps him position himself correctly for both defensive and attacking shots.

Mental Toughness

His mental strength is crucial, especially in high-pressure situations. Kohli remains calm under pressure, often finishing networth with crucial knocks when chasing targets. His competitive spirit drives him to exceed limits and maintain high standards for himself and his team.

Fitness and Athleticism

Kohli’s exceptional fitness contributes significantly to his performance. His agility and swift running between wickets are key aspects of his game, enhancing his overall effectiveness on the field.
